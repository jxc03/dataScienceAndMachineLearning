## Key concepts from the lecture
## 1. Big data: more than "a big file"
**Big Data is often explained via the 4 V's:**
- **Volume** (how much)
- **Velocity** (how fast it arrives/changes)
- **Variety** (different formats/types)
- **Veracity** (quality/uncertainty/noise)

**Important idea:** A dataset can look "big" but still be insufficient if the **feature space is huge**. Example from the slides: with **20 boolean variables**, you’d need up to **2^20 = 1,048,576** rows to represent all combinations.

## 2. Instances/Rows & Variables/Columns"
**The different types of variables:**
- **Categorical/nominal/factors** 
- **Strings** 
- **Continuous**
- **Discrete**

**Note that:** Columns/variables are also typically called features or attributes
